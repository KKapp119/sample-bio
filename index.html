Research Focus: Microplastics

My primary research investigates the prevalence and distribution of microplastic pollution in freshwater systems, challenging the assumption that remote environments are untouched by human contaminants.

  • Greater Yellowstone Ecosystem: My studies have documented significant microplastic contamination in the Snake River and other high-elevation freshwater bodies previously considered pristine.
  • Sources & Transport: Our findings suggest that atmospheric deposition plays a major role in transporting microplastics to remote alpine environments.
  • Citizen Science & Education: A cornerstone of this research is involving CWC undergraduates and citizen scientists in data collection, providing critical field experience while contributing to a growing global dataset on freshwater plastic pollution.
